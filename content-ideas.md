# snow-globe — Content Ideas

*The vibe: imagine someone with impeccable taste just left their TV on and you walked into the room.*

## Lectures & Talks
- Old Bell Labs films (incredible 50s-60s science films)
- James Burke's *Connections* — the original "everything is related to everything"
- Buckminster Fuller's marathon lectures
- Feynman's messenger lectures (the New Zealand ones)
- Alan Watts talks with ambient visuals

## Weird Educational
- How It's Made — the obscure episodes (pencils, harmonicas, glass eyes)
- Old Open University BBC broadcasts (70s/80s British professors just... teaching)
- Soviet-era educational films (subtitled — wild aesthetics)
- NHK documentaries — Japan makes the most beautiful, patient docs on earth
- The Machine That Changed the World (1992 BBC computing documentary)

## Art / Music
- Tiny Desk Concerts (the ones with 50k views, not 5 million)
- Old live performances — Nina Simone at Montreux, Coltrane in '66
- Video art — Nam June Paik, Bill Viola
- People making things — blacksmithing, bookbinding, pottery, luthiers

## Ethnographic / Anthropological
- David Attenborough's early stuff before Planet Earth
- Old National Geographic specials
- Disappearing World (Granada TV, indigenous cultures filmed in the 70s-80s)
- Prelinger Archives — mid-century American ephemeral films

## Liminal / Ambient
- Train window footage from around the world
- Slow TV (Norwegian train rides, fireplace, boat journeys)
- City walks — Tokyo at 4am, Istanbul bazaars, rainy Seoul
- ISS live feed footage

## Genuinely Strange
- Chris Marker — La Jetée (28 min, will ruin you)
- Jan Švankmajer's stop-motion (Czech surrealist animation)
- Sesame Street clips from the 70s (genuinely avant-garde)
- Mathematical Cranks — conference talks by unconventional theorists

## Anna's Picks
- Old cartoons
- Richard Feynman lectures
- Famous/great lectures
- Nature footage — ocean, forests
- Ethnographic stuff
